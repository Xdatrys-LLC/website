Europe

35 locations
#01
Oia, Santorini
Greece
White cubic houses stack against deep cobalt sea. The blue domes glow at golden hour. Shoot from above the caldera steps in the last 30 minutes before sunset.
#02
Gamla Stan
Sweden — Stockholm
Medieval cobblestones and ochre facades form natural leading lines. The narrow alleys of Köpmantorget compress beautifully into a 3x telephoto frame.
#03
Riomaggiore, Cinque Terre
Italy
Pastel houses cascade down a cliff into the Ligurian Sea. Shoot from the harbour breakwater at blue hour for reflections in the still water below.
#04
Hallstatt Village
Austria
The lake reflects the Alps perfectly on still mornings before 8am. Shoot from the boat landing opposite the village for the classic full-reflection frame.
#05
Charles Bridge at Dawn
Czech Republic — Prague
Bridge statues emerge from morning mist with the illuminated castle behind. Arrive an hour before sunrise to claim a position before the crowds arrive.
#06
Jemaa el-Fna Square
Morocco — Marrakech
Smoke, lantern light, and a thousand people in motion at dusk. Switch to Night Mode just before full dark to hold the orange glow without blowing out the highlights.
#07
Trocadéro, Eiffel Tower View
France — Paris
The only spot where the tower fills the frame symmetrically. Arrive at blue hour for the tower lights against a deep cobalt sky before the crowds gather.
#08
Colosseum at Sunrise
Italy — Rome
Warm amber light on ancient travertine stone with no crowds. The Arch of Constantine creates a natural foreground frame from the south approach.
#09
Lauterbrunnen Valley
Switzerland
Seventy-two waterfalls drop from sheer cliff faces into one green valley. Shoot the Staubbach Falls with the village below for a strong sense of scale.
#10
Dubrovnik Old Town Walls
Croatia
Walk the medieval walls at golden hour when terracotta rooftops glow and the Adriatic turns deep blue. Use 3x telephoto to compress the roof layers.
#11
Trolltunga Rock
Norway
A rock shelf suspended 700m above a glacial lake. One person standing on the ledge against the valley below creates a powerful sense of scale.
#12
Alberobello Trulli
Italy — Puglia
Conical limestone rooftops spread across the hillside. Shoot from the Rione Monti overlook in late afternoon when shadows define each individual cone.
#13
Bruges Canal Reflections
Belgium
Medieval brick bridges and gabled houses reflect in the still canal water. The Rozenhoedkaai corner is the classic shot at dusk when the lights come on.
#14
Chefchaouen Blue City
Morocco
Every wall, staircase, and doorway painted in varying shades of blue. The colour contrast with terracotta pots and orange cats is pure visual richness.
#15
Plitvice Lakes
Croatia
Cascading turquoise lakes connected by wooden walkways through forest. The Veliki Slap waterfall from the viewpoint above is the signature shot.
#16
Skógafoss Waterfall
Iceland
A 60-metre curtain of white water. Stand close for the mist rainbow at midday, or climb the staircase for scale against the flat volcanic plain below.
#17
Valletta Grand Harbour
Malta
The most fortified harbour in the world, rimmed with honey-coloured baroque buildings. Shoot from the Upper Barrakka Gardens at the moment of sunset.
#18
Ronda El Tajo Gorge
Spain
A city on the edge of a 100-metre gorge. The Puente Nuevo bridge spans the gap. Shoot from the path below for the full drama of the drop.
#19
Burano Island
Italy — Venice
Every house painted a different primary colour. Shoot down the canal for reflections that turn the water into an abstract painting of pure colour.
#20
Reynisfjara Black Beach
Iceland
Black sand, black basalt columns, white waves, grey sky. A monochrome drama unlike anywhere else. Long exposure blurs the surf into smooth silk.
#21
Heidelberg Castle View
Germany
The ruined castle commands the Neckar Valley. Shoot from the Philosophers' Walk across the river at golden hour for warm-lit ruins against deep blue sky.
#22
Porto Ribeira Waterfront
Portugal
Azulejo-tiled houses line the Douro River with wine lodges opposite. Shoot from the Dom Luís bridge at dusk when the reflections appear in the water.
#23
Piazza San Marco Flooded
Italy — Venice
When acqua alta floods the square, the basilica reflects across the entire piazza. A once-in-a-visit opportunity that most tourists miss by sheltering indoors.
#24
Meteora Monasteries
Greece
Orthodox monasteries on top of vast grey rock pillars. Shoot at sunrise before the tour buses arrive, from the road below looking straight up.
#25
Sintra Pena Palace
Portugal
A Romanticist castle in vivid yellow and red above the forest canopy. Misty mornings turn the surrounding forest into something from a fairy tale.
#26
Amalfi Cliff Road
Italy
The road clings to a cliff above the Tyrrhenian Sea. Shoot down into the white villages from the cliff-road bends high above for a vertiginous perspective.
#27
Lake Bled Island Church
Slovenia
A lone church on a lake island with the Julian Alps behind. The classic shot from Ojstrica viewpoint rewards a 20-minute hike with a perfect frame.
#28
Seljalandsfoss Walk-Behind
Iceland
You walk behind this waterfall and shoot outward through the water curtain against the golden Icelandic sky. Bring a waterproof case for the phone.
#29
Ghent Graslei at Night
Belgium
Medieval guild houses reflected in the Leie River, lit gold at night. The night shot from the opposite bank rivals anything in Western Europe.
#30
Kotor Bay from the Walls
Montenegro
Climb the fortification walls above the old town for a telephoto view of the bay and surrounding limestone mountains compressed into one frame.
#31
Palermo Street Markets
Italy — Sicily
Ballarò and Vucciria markets overflow with hanging meat, spice pyramids, and street food. Shoot at 3x to compress the layers of colour and texture.
#32
Biarritz Grande Plage
France
Belle époque hotels frame a broad Atlantic beach with surfers. The Casino terrace gives an elevated view over the beach that most visitors never find.
#33
Gorges du Verdon
France
Turquoise river 700 metres below sheer limestone walls. The Belvedère de la Dent d'Aire gives the most dramatic elevated view available by road.
#34
Algarve Sea Caves
Portugal
Orange limestone arches at Ponta da Piedade glow against turquoise water. Kayak through for interior shots of light filtering through the cave mouth.
#35
Spreewald Canals
Germany
Narrow canals through birch forest, navigated by punt. The still water reflections in autumn produce extraordinary colour and symmetry.

Asia & Middle East

28 locations
#36
Fushimi Inari Torii Gates
Japan — Kyoto
Ten thousand vermilion torii gates form tunnels up the mountainside. Arrive before 7am for empty gates and warm red light before the crowds arrive.
#37
Erg Chebbi Dunes
Morocco — Merzouga
The vast Saharan dune field turns every shade of orange at sunrise. A camel caravan silhouette against the dune crest at first light is the iconic shot.
#38
Pachetia Hill, Jodhpur Blue City
India — Rajasthan
An unbroken cobalt carpet of blue houses below with Mehrangarh Fort rising at the northern edge. Best at golden hour from the hilltop viewpoint.
#39
Ha Long Bay Karst Islands
Vietnam
Two thousand limestone islands rise from jade-green water in morning mist. Shoot from the bow of a junk boat at sunrise when the mist is thickest.
#40
Shibuya Crossing
Japan — Tokyo
The world's busiest pedestrian crossing. Shoot from the Starbucks window above for the overhead view of the human flow pattern below.
#41
Taj Mahal at Dawn
India — Agra
The marble changes from grey to rose to white as the sun rises. Arrive at the east gate opening for the light and the empty foreground pool reflection.
#42
Shwedagon Pagoda
Myanmar — Yangon
The golden stupa glows electric against a dark sky at night. Circle the terraces for different angles of the gilded spires and their reflections below.
#43
Sheikh Zayed Grand Mosque
UAE — Abu Dhabi
The white marble facade reflects in the surrounding pool at blue hour after sunset, when the moon-tuned lighting turns the marble electric white.
#44
Bagan Temple Plains
Myanmar
Over two thousand ancient temples spread across a dusty plain. Shoot from a balloon or the taller temples at sunrise as the mist burns off slowly.
#45
Arashiyama Bamboo Grove
Japan — Kyoto
Bamboo walls close overhead into a green tunnel. Shoot at 9am on a weekday. The canopy creates diffused emerald light that the phone handles beautifully.
#46
Wadi Rum Desert Valley
Jordan
Red sandstone mountains rise from the desert floor at extraordinary scale. At sunset the rocks turn deep crimson. Night Mode captures the Milky Way above.
#47
Petra Treasury at Dusk
Jordan
The rose-red carved facade glows in the narrow siq canyon at midday. The candlelit night event turns it into pure magic worth the extra ticket price.
#48
Chureito Pagoda with Fuji
Japan
Five-storey pagoda frames Mount Fuji above Fujiyoshida town. Cherry blossom season (late March to April) adds pink clouds to the foreground.
#49
Hoi An Ancient Town
Vietnam
Lantern-lit streets at night create a warm amber glow. The Thu Bon River reflects the lanterns double. The monthly Lantern Festival maximises the intensity.
#50
Bali Rice Terraces, Tegallalang
Indonesia
Stepped green terraces catch morning light in layers of shadow and highlight. Shoot from the village road above at 10am for the best light angle.
#51
Dubai Frame at Night
UAE
The 150-metre picture frame shows old Dubai through one side and new Dubai through the other. Shoot from across the park for the full symmetry.
#52
Cappadocia Hot Air Balloons
Turkey
Hundreds of balloons rise above the fairy chimney landscape at sunrise. No other view on earth looks quite like this. Book six months ahead.
#53
Marrakech Souk Spice Market
Morocco
Pyramids of saffron, cumin, and rose petals catch the filtered souk light. Crouch low for a ground-level shot through the spice mounds toward the light source.
#54
Phi Phi Islands Viewpoint
Thailand
The Maya Bay viewpoint frames the curved bay between limestone cliffs. Arrive on the first morning boat before the day crowds get there.
#55
Kathmandu Durbar Square
Nepal
Ancient pagoda temples crowd together with traders, pilgrims, and pigeons. The wide angle captures layered temple rooflines against the Himalayan sky.
#56
Borobudur at Sunrise
Indonesia — Java
Buddhist stupas emerge from volcanic mist as dawn breaks. The fog burns off slowly. Arrive two hours before sunrise to secure a position on the upper terrace.
#57
Maldives Water Villas
Maldives
Overwater bungalows extend across lagoons of impossible turquoise. The sunrise shot from the end of the jetty with bungalows receding into the distance.
#58
Havana Malecon at Sunset
Cuba
The seafront promenade is lined with crumbling pastel buildings and vintage American cars. The warm evening light turns everything amber. Shoot toward the sun.
#59
Luang Prabang Alms Giving
Laos
Saffron-robed monks file through the streets at dawn collecting alms. Shoot from a respectful distance with the longest optical zoom. Never use flash.
#60
Sigiriya Rock Fortress
Sri Lanka
A 200-metre granite monolith rises from the jungle plain. The view from the summit at sunrise shows flat jungle canopy stretching to every horizon.
#61
Shinjuku Neon at Night
Japan — Tokyo
The densest concentration of illuminated signs on earth. Rain makes the pavements reflective. The wet street reflection shot is the one every visitor wants.
#62
Havelock Island Beach
Andaman Islands, India
Radhanagar Beach curves into turquoise water with no visible development. Pure blue and white geometry that the phone renders faithfully in any light.
#63
Lijiang Old Town Rooftops
China — Yunnan
Grey-tiled Naxi rooftops layer across the hillside with Jade Dragon Snow Mountain behind. Shoot from the hilltop park for the full roofscape panorama.

The Americas

20 locations
#64
El Pípila Viewpoint, Guanajuato
Mexico
A kaleidoscopic bowl of colonial facades in terracotta, cerulean, and lime stacked up canyon hillsides. Switch to ultrawide and tap a mid-shadow zone to set exposure.
#65
Galápagos Wildlife Encounters
Ecuador
The animals have no fear of humans. Blue-footed boobies and marine iguanas let you approach to arm's reach. The wildlife portraits here are extraordinary.
#66
Uyuni Salt Flat at Dawn
Bolivia
After rain, the world's largest salt flat becomes a perfect mirror. The sky doubles below your feet and the horizon disappears entirely.
#67
Torres del Paine, Paine Towers
Chile — Patagonia
Three granite towers reflected in Laguna Torres at dawn. The reflection is best after the 2.5-hour hike, when the wind drops and the lake settles to stillness.
#68
Iguazu Falls, Devil's Throat
Argentina and Brazil
A U-shaped chasm with 275 falls pouring into white chaos below. The walkway right above Devil's Throat puts you inside the roar and the spray.
#69
Old Havana Street Life
Cuba
Crumbling beauty on every corner. Vintage Chevrolets parked beneath baroque columns. The contrast between architectural ruin and human pride is the whole story.
#70
Antelope Canyon Light Beams
USA — Arizona
Shafts of light penetrate the slot canyon between 11am and 1pm and illuminate swirling sandstone walls. Book a guided tour for access to the best beam positions.
#71
Machu Picchu at Sunrise
Peru
The citadel emerges from cloud forest as the morning mist lifts. Book the first entry slot and position on the Sun Gate for the classic approach shot.
#72
Horseshoe Bend, Colorado River
USA — Arizona
The river curves 300 metres below in a perfect horseshoe. Shoot vertically, as the shot is taller than it is wide. Arrive at golden hour for warm canyon walls.
#73
Oahu Sunrise from Diamond Head
USA — Hawaii
The extinct volcanic crater gives an elevated view over Waikiki and the Pacific. Arrive 90 minutes before sunrise and claim a position before the path fills.
#74
Cartagena Walled City
Colombia
Painted colonial houses with flowering balconies in a compact walled city. The warm evening light turns the yellow, blue, and pink facades luminous.
#75
New York Brooklyn Bridge View
USA — New York
The Manhattan skyline framed through the bridge cables from DUMBO. Early morning gives the clean shot before the crowds and the scaffolding appear.
#76
Perito Moreno Glacier
Argentina — Patagonia
A 60-metre wall of blue ice advancing into Lago Argentino. The colour is a deep electric blue that the phone camera renders faithfully in flat overcast light.
#77
Lake Atitlán, Panajachel
Guatemala
Three volcanoes reflected in a caldera lake below indigenous Maya villages. Pre-dawn light turns the lake surface to hammered silver.
#78
Amazon Riverine Sunrise
Brazil and Peru
The Amazon at dawn from a boat: pink light on still water, river dolphins, and jungle stretching to the horizon. The scale humbles every camera.
#79
Death Valley Badwater Basin
USA — California
The lowest point in North America forms a cracked salt flat extending to mountain walls on all sides. Shoot at sunrise or sunset only. Midday is dangerous.
#80
Tobacco Bay, Bermuda
Bermuda
The most extraordinary turquoise water accessible from a standard beach. The clarity makes underwater photography possible with a waterproof phone case.
#81
Central Park in Autumn
USA — New York
The Bow Bridge over the Lake in October. Golden foliage reflected in still water with the skyscrapers rising behind. Shoot from the Ladies' Pavilion.
#82
Palenque Jungle Ruins
Mexico — Chiapas
Maya temples emerging from dense jungle rather than cleared ground. Morning mist at 8am creates a mystical atmosphere that the afternoon completely loses.
#83
Morro de São Paulo Beach
Brazil — Bahia
Car-free island beaches with coloured wooden fishing boats and turquoise water. Shoot from the lighthouse hill above the village for the full panorama.

Africa & Middle East

10 locations
#84
Serengeti Great Migration
Tanzania
A million wildebeest crossing the Mara River. Use a long telephoto lens to compress the herds into a single frame. July to September is the peak crossing season.
#85
Sossusvlei Dunes, Dead Vlei
Namibia
Ancient skeletal trees in a white clay pan surrounded by 300-metre orange dunes. The dead trees have stood for 900 years. Shoot at first light before the heat haze builds.
#86
Zanzibar Stone Town
Tanzania
Carved wooden doors and crumbling coral-stone architecture in an Arab-Swahili old town. Narrow alleys create dramatic shadow patterns at midday.
#87
Cape of Good Hope
South Africa
The Atlantic and Indian Oceans appear to meet at this rocky headland. Shoot toward the lighthouse from the lower path for dramatic cliff-top scale.
#88
Victoria Falls at Full Flood
Zimbabwe and Zambia
The world's largest waterfall sheet creates a permanent mist cloud visible 50km away. The knife-edge bridge over the gorge is the classic vantage point.
#89
Sahara Star Trails
Morocco — Merzouga
The darkest night skies in North Africa above the Erg Chebbi dunes. A camel silhouette against the Milky Way is achievable with Night Mode on any modern phone.
#90
Lamu Old Town
Kenya
The best-preserved Swahili settlement in East Africa. No cars, only donkeys and boats. Narrow coral-stone alleys glow warm in the afternoon sun.
#91
Table Mountain From Below
South Africa — Cape Town
The flat-topped mountain frames the city in every direction. Shoot from the Bo-Kaap neighbourhood with coloured houses in the foreground and the mountain behind.
#92
Danakil Depression
Ethiopia
The most alien landscape on earth. Acid lakes in yellow, green, and orange surround active lava lakes. Accessible only with a specialist guide.
#93
Essaouira Blue Fishing Port
Morocco
Indigo-painted fishing boats in a medieval walled Atlantic port. The constant strong wind creates dramatic cloud movements above the ramparts.

Oceania & Pacific

8 locations
#94
Sydney Opera House at Dawn
Australia
The harbour glows pink and the sail roofs catch the first light before any crowds arrive. Shoot from Mrs Macquarie's Chair headland across the water.
#95
Milford Sound, Mitre Peak
New Zealand
The 1,692-metre peak rises sheer from the fjord surface. Shoot from the cruise boat at dawn when the reflection is still and rainfall creates temporary waterfalls.
#96
Bora Bora Lagoon Overwater
French Polynesia
The turquoise lagoon around Mount Otemanu is the definitive Pacific island shot. The overwater bungalow jetty at dawn, before any guests appear, is the clean version.
#97
Waitomo Glowworm Caves
New Zealand
The cave ceiling covered in thousands of bioluminescent larvae creates a blue star field underground. Long exposure on Night Mode from the boat without flash.
#98
Uluru at Sunrise and Sunset
Australia — Northern Territory
The red sandstone monolith changes from grey to orange to crimson as the light shifts. Shoot from the sunrise viewing area, then stay for the full colour transition.
#99
Fiji Yasawa Islands
Fiji
Remote islands accessible only by seaplane with white sand beaches and crystal visibility. Snorkelling photographs are extraordinary with the phone in a waterproof case.
#100
Whitsundays Whitehaven Beach
Australia — Queensland
The swirling white silica sand patterns at Hill Inlet change with every tide. Shoot from the Tongue Point lookout for the aerial-view swirl pattern.
#101
Aoraki Mount Cook at Night
New Zealand
The Southern Alps in New Zealand's darkest skies give the most extraordinary Milky Way arching over snow-capped peaks. Night Mode with a fence post for stability.
